The Piece

A real lavender rests on a pedestal beside a screen. Touch it, and a second lavender — a constellation of light — moves through the stages of its existence.

Touch and Bloom is an interactive installation that translates a gesture into a life cycle. When a viewer touches the plant, a capacitive sensor reads the contact and advances the digital lavender through three stages of its existence: sprout, seedling, bloom.

Three Stages of Existence
01

Sprout

The first coherence. A handful of points agree to become a stem — barely more than an intention.

02

Seedling

Structure appears. Leaves reach outward, the stem commits to a direction. The form learns to hold itself.

03

Bloom

Full presence. Violet spikes of light — a lavender that exists only as long as it is held.

In the field, this takes months. Here, it takes seconds — but only with your hand.

State 00 — Zero Gravity

When no one touches, the form dissolves. Weightless. Suspended between forms — neither one stage nor another.

A touch returns them to coherence.

You are the season,
the rain,
the sun.

The plant cannot grow without being touched — only with your participation.

What is the relationship between a living thing and the time of its existence?

How It's Made

  • Arduino microcontroller + capacitive touch sensor — reads contact with the living plant
  • Custom point-cloud rendering engine — built in p5.js
  • Photographs of Lavandula angustifolia converted into three-dimensional clouds of light
  • Point-cloud morphing — a technique after media artist Christian Brinkmann, here turned toward touch, time, and growth
